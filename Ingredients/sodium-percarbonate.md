# Sodium Percarbonate (Oxygen Bleach)

> A granular adduct of sodium carbonate and hydrogen peroxide that releases active oxygen (H₂O₂) when dissolved in water, providing effective color-safe bleaching, stain removal, and sanitization without chlorine — the active ingredient in OxiClean and most commercial "oxygen bleach" products.

| Appearance | White, free-flowing granular powder; odorless |

## Overview

Sodium percarbonate is an addition compound (adduct) of sodium carbonate (washing soda, Na₂CO₃) and hydrogen peroxide (H₂O₂) in a 2:3 molar ratio. When dissolved in water, it dissociates cleanly into its two components: the sodium carbonate provides alkalinity (boosting cleaning and stain removal) while the hydrogen peroxide provides active oxygen for bleaching and disinfection. It is, in essence, a solid, shelf-stable form of hydrogen peroxide combined with a built-in alkaline booster.

This dual-action chemistry makes sodium percarbonate dramatically more effective than liquid 3% hydrogen peroxide for laundry and cleaning applications. While a 32 oz bottle of 3% H₂O₂ contains only about 28 mL of actual hydrogen peroxide, a tablespoon of sodium percarbonate dissolved in warm water releases H₂O₂ at effective bleaching concentrations (~1,000–3,000 ppm active oxygen) in an alkaline solution that greatly enhances its oxidative power. The alkaline pH is critical: hydrogen peroxide is a far more effective bleach above pH 9 because the perhydroxyl anion (HOO⁻) is a more potent oxidant than molecular H₂O₂.

Sodium percarbonate is the primary active ingredient in OxiClean, Vanish, and most "oxygen bleach" products sold worldwide. The commercial versions simply add surfactants and chelating agents to the base compound. For the DIY household, buying pure sodium percarbonate in bulk provides identical chemistry at a fraction of the cost.

Environmentally, sodium percarbonate is exemplary: it decomposes into sodium carbonate (a natural mineral), water, and oxygen. No chlorine, no persistent residues, no toxic byproducts.

## Chemical Composition & Structure

### Decomposition in Water

When sodium percarbonate dissolves:

2Na₂CO₃·3H₂O₂ → 2Na₂CO₃ + 3H₂O₂

The released H₂O₂ then acts as an oxidizing bleach:

H₂O₂ → H₂O + ½O₂ (catalytic decomposition)
H₂O₂ + Stain(colored) → Stain(colorless) + H₂O (oxidative bleaching)

### Active Oxygen Content

- Sodium percarbonate contains approximately **13.5% active oxygen** by weight
- This is the standard measure of bleaching power for oxygen bleaches
- For comparison: commercial OxiClean contains sodium percarbonate as its primary ingredient plus sodium carbonate filler and surfactants

### Why Alkaline pH Matters

At pH ~10.5 (the natural pH of sodium percarbonate solution):
- H₂O₂ partially dissociates: H₂O₂ ⇌ HOO⁻ + H⁺
- The perhydroxyl anion (HOO⁻) is a **much more powerful** nucleophilic oxidant than H₂O₂
- This is why oxygen bleach works better in warm/hot water with alkaline pH
- Acidic conditions (mixing with vinegar) would neutralize the carbonate and reduce bleaching power

### Temperature Dependence

| Temperature | Activation | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Cold (<30°C / 86°F) | Slow; incomplete activation | Pre-soak (longer contact time) |
| Warm (40–50°C / 104–122°F) | Good activation | Standard laundry, general cleaning |
| Hot (60°C+ / 140°F+) | Rapid, full activation | Heavy stains, sanitization, whitening |

### Physical Properties

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Melting point | Decomposes ~50–60°C (releases oxygen; does not truly melt) |
| Density | ~0.9–1.1 g/cm³ (bulk powder) |
| Stability | Stable when dry; degrades slowly in humid conditions |
| Flash point | Non-flammable, but is an oxidizer |

## How It Is Produced / How to Make It from Scratch

### Industrial Production

1. **Spray-drying method**: A solution of sodium carbonate and hydrogen peroxide is spray-dried, forming granules as the water evaporates
2. **Crystallization method**: Sodium carbonate is dissolved in concentrated hydrogen peroxide solution; the adduct crystallizes out
3. **Coating**: Industrial grades may be coated with sodium silicate or other stabilizers to improve shelf life and prevent premature decomposition

### Home Production

Not practical or safe. Concentrating hydrogen peroxide is dangerous (decomposition is exothermic; concentrated H₂O₂ is a powerful oxidizer). Buy commercially produced sodium percarbonate.

### Relationship to Other Bleaches

| Bleach | Active Agent | pH | Color-Safe | Residue |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sodium percarbonate | H₂O₂ (active oxygen) | ~10.5 | Yes (at recommended dose) | Na₂CO₃ + H₂O + O₂ |
| Sodium hypochlorite (chlorine bleach) | HOCl | ~12 | No (strips dyes) | NaCl + H₂O |
| Sodium perborate | H₃BO₃ + H₂O₂ | ~10 | Yes | Boron residue |
| Hydrogen peroxide (3%) | H₂O₂ | ~4 | Yes | H₂O + O₂ |

## Mechanism of Action

### Oxidative Bleaching

1. Dissolved sodium percarbonate releases H₂O₂ into alkaline solution
2. At pH >9, significant HOO⁻ (perhydroxyl anion) forms
3. HOO⁻ attacks conjugated double-bond systems in colored stain molecules (chromophores)
4. Oxidative cleavage breaks the chromophore, rendering the molecule colorless
5. The stain is chemically destroyed, not just moved or masked

### Stain Specificity

Sodium percarbonate is most effective against **organic stains**:
- **Blood**: Oxidizes heme (iron-porphyrin complex), breaking the colored compound
- **Wine/berry**: Oxidizes anthocyanin pigments
- **Coffee/tea**: Oxidizes tannin-melanoidin complexes
- **Grass**: Oxidizes chlorophyll and protein
- **Sweat yellowing**: Oxidizes sebum-protein complexes
- **Food stains**: Most organic food pigments are susceptible to oxidative bleaching

Less effective against:
- Rust stains (use oxalic acid)
- Dye transfer (may help but not always sufficient)
- Set-in grease (use washing soda paste first)

### Sanitization

At effective concentrations in warm water:
- H₂O₂ destroys bacterial cell membranes through oxidation
- Effective against common household bacteria, mold, and mildew
- Not as rapid or potent as chlorine bleach for disinfection, but adequate for laundry and household sanitization

### Deodorization

- Oxidizes odor-causing organic compounds rather than masking them
- Effective against musty/mildew odors, sweat odor in fabrics, organic decomposition odors

## Applications & Uses

### In DIY Formulations

**Oxygen Bleach for Whites (Primary Use)**:
- ½ cup sodium percarbonate per load of white laundry
- Use warm/hot water (≥40°C) for best activation
- Soak for 1–6 hours for heavily stained items
- Whitens without the damage or chlorine smell of bleach

**Pre-Soak for Stains**:
- Dissolve 1–2 tbsp in 1 gallon warm water
- Soak stained garment for 1–6 hours (overnight for set-in stains)
- Blood, wine, coffee, sweat yellowing, food stains
- Wash as normal after soaking

**Laundry Sanitizer**:
- Add ½ cup to hot wash cycle
- Kills bacteria and mold in towels, sheets, workout clothes
- Particularly useful for cloth diapers and musty towels

**Grout and Tile Cleaner**:
- Make paste with warm water
- Apply to grout, let sit 15–30 minutes
- Scrub and rinse — whitens grout and removes mold staining

**Deck and Patio Cleaner**:
- Dissolve 1 cup in 1 gallon warm water
- Apply to wood deck or concrete
- Scrub with stiff brush, rinse with hose
- Removes algae, mildew, and organic staining

**Toilet Bowl Cleaner**:
- Sprinkle 2–3 tbsp in toilet bowl
- Let sit 30 minutes to overnight
- Scrub — removes organic staining and sanitizes

**Carpet Stain Removal**:
- Dissolve 1 tbsp in 1 cup warm water
- Apply to carpet stain, blot, repeat
- Test in inconspicuous area first (color-safe but test anyway)

**Dishwasher Boost**:
- Add 1 tbsp to dishwasher along with regular detergent
- Removes tea/coffee staining from mugs
- Helps with cloudy glassware

## Efficacy & Research

### Bleaching Performance

- Coons, D.M. et al. (2005) in *Journal of Surfactants and Detergents* demonstrated that sodium percarbonate-based oxygen bleaches effectively removed a range of common household stains at temperatures ≥40°C, with performance approaching that of sodium hypochlorite (chlorine bleach) at temperatures ≥60°C for most organic stains.

### Antimicrobial Activity

- Sodium percarbonate solutions at recommended laundry concentrations have been shown to reduce bacterial loads by >99% in hot wash conditions (≥60°C) and >90% in warm wash (40°C), per studies on hospital laundry decontamination.

### Color Safety

- At recommended concentrations (150–300 ppm active oxygen), sodium percarbonate does not cause significant color loss in most dyed fabrics. Concentrated solutions or prolonged contact can fade sensitive dyes.

## Safety, Warnings & Contraindications

### Oxidizer — Handle with Care

- Sodium percarbonate is classified as an **oxidizer** (GHS oxidizing solid, Category 3)
- Do not store near flammable materials, reducing agents, or organic solvents
- Do not mix with concentrated acids (rapid decomposition, heat, spattering)
- Avoid contact with metals (especially iron, copper, manganese) which catalyze rapid H₂O₂ decomposition

### Critical Incompatibilities

- **Do NOT mix with chlorine bleach**: Unnecessary and can cause dangerous reactions
- **Do NOT mix with vinegar or citric acid**: Neutralizes the alkaline component, reducing effectiveness; rapid gas evolution possible
- **Do NOT mix with ammonia**: Mixing with H₂O₂ in alkaline conditions can create irritating vapors
- **Do NOT use on silk, wool, or leather**: Alkaline oxidizer damages protein fibers

### Handling

- Wear gloves when handling concentrated solutions (skin irritant)
- Avoid eye contact; flush with water if contact occurs
- Store in sealed container away from moisture (humidity causes slow decomposition)
- Keep away from children and pets

### Fabric Compatibility

- **Safe on**: Cotton, polyester, nylon, linen, most synthetics, most colored fabrics (at recommended concentration)
- **NOT safe on**: Silk, wool, leather, some delicate dyes (especially natural dyes), vintage/antique fabrics
- Always test on inconspicuous area for valued items

## Pros & Cons

### Advantages

- Far more effective than liquid 3% H₂O₂ for laundry and cleaning
- Color-safe at recommended concentrations
- Decomposes to harmless sodium carbonate, water, and oxygen
- No chlorine smell or residue
- Whitens without degrading fabric (unlike chlorine bleach)
- Effective stain remover, sanitizer, and deodorizer
- Shelf-stable powder (unlike liquid H₂O₂)
- Very cost-effective in bulk
- Works with the alkaline laundry system (washing soda, borax, soap)
- Replaces multiple commercial products

### Disadvantages / Limitations

- Requires warm/hot water for full activation (ineffective in cold water)
- Not as powerful as chlorine bleach for disinfection/whitening
- Damages protein fibers (silk, wool)
- Classified as oxidizer — storage precautions needed
- Degrades in humid storage conditions
- Can irritate skin and eyes in concentrated solutions
- Not effective on rust or mineral stains (use oxalic acid or citric acid)
- Pre-dissolve before adding to wash for best results

## Storage & Shelf Life

- **Shelf life**: 1–2 years in sealed, dry storage; degrades with moisture exposure
- **Storage**: Cool, dry place in airtight container. Original bag or sealed plastic bucket.
- **Humidity sensitivity**: Absorbs moisture and slowly decomposes, releasing oxygen. May cause package to swell if sealed in humid conditions.
- **Signs of degradation**: Loss of fizzing when dissolved (no active oxygen remaining)
- **Fire safety**: Store away from flammable materials; it is an oxidizer
- **Container**: Do not store in metal containers (catalyzes decomposition)

- **EPA (USA)**: Listed as an antimicrobial pesticide with exemptions for certain uses.
- **FDA**: Components (Na₂CO₃ and H₂O₂) are both GRAS individually.
- **EU**: Approved component in cleaning products. Subject to CLP regulation as oxidizer.
- **GHS Classification**: Oxidizing solid, Category 3 (H272); Eye irritation (H319); Skin irritation (H315).

## Related Ingredients

- **Hydrogen Peroxide (3%)** — The liquid form of the active bleaching agent. Less effective for laundry because it's too dilute and acidic. Sodium percarbonate delivers H₂O₂ at higher concentration in alkaline solution.
- **Washing Soda (Sodium Carbonate)** — One of the decomposition products. Complementary: washing soda handles grease, sodium percarbonate handles organic stains and whitening.
- **Borax** — Alkaline laundry booster. Works alongside sodium percarbonate in a complete laundry system.
- **Oxalic Acid** — For rust and tannin stains that oxygen bleach cannot address.
- **White Vinegar** — Do NOT mix directly. Use vinegar in rinse cycle only, after wash cycle containing sodium percarbonate.
